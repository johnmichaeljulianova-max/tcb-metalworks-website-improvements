Structural Steel Fabrication for a Commercial Construction Project

By TCB Metal Works Racine, Wisconsin Project Case Study

Project at a Glance

A commercial construction client needed custom structural steel components fabricated to strict engineering specifications and ready for efficient site installation. TCB Metal Works combined structural steel fabrication, precision welding, and custom metal fabrication with strict quality control to deliver parts built to spec.

Introduction

Structural steel is the backbone of many commercial and industrial developments. For this project, TCB Metal Works was engaged to fabricate custom structural steel components designed to meet strict engineering specifications while supporting efficient installation on-site.

From precision cutting and welding to quality inspections, every stage of fabrication was completed with a focus on durability, accuracy, and long-term performance.

The Challenge

Commercial construction projects demand structural components that fit perfectly, perform reliably, and meet project schedules.

The client required fabricated steel members that would:

  • Meet engineering specifications
  • Maintain dimensional accuracy
  • Support heavy structural loads
  • Allow efficient on-site installation
  • Deliver long-term durability

Any inaccuracies during fabrication could result in costly installation delays.

Our Solution

TCB Metal Works manufactured each structural component using proven fabrication methods and strict quality control procedures.

The project involved:

  • Structural steel cutting
  • Precision welding
  • Plate fabrication
  • Beam preparation
  • Dimensional verification
  • Final inspection

Each fabricated component was inspected before delivery to ensure compliance with project requirements.

Engineering Considerations

Structural steel fabrication requires more than simply joining steel together.

Our team focused on:

  • Dimensional accuracy
  • Weld consistency
  • Structural integrity
  • Material quality
  • Corrosion resistance
  • Ease of installation

These considerations help reduce installation time while ensuring reliable long-term performance.

The Results

The completed structural steel package was delivered ready for installation, providing the client with fabricated components manufactured to specification and designed for dependable service.

This project highlights TCB Metal Works' capability to support commercial construction through high-quality structural steel fabrication and custom manufacturing solutions.

Why Choose TCB Metal Works

Our team combines fabrication experience, precision workmanship, and quality materials to manufacture structural steel solutions that meet demanding commercial and industrial requirements.

Whether producing individual components or complete fabricated assemblies, we focus on delivering products built for strength, accuracy, and long-term performance.

Frequently Asked Questions

What types of structural steel projects do you fabricate?

We fabricate a broad range of structural steel — beams, columns, plate assemblies, frames, and custom load-bearing components for commercial, industrial, and specialized projects. Each job is built to the specifications supplied for it rather than from a fixed catalogue.

Can you manufacture from engineering drawings?

Yes. We fabricate directly from supplied engineering drawings and specifications, and we can help develop or refine details when you are working from a concept rather than finished plans.

Do you provide custom steel fabrication?

Yes. Custom fabrication is central to what we do — components and assemblies built to your exact requirements rather than adapted from stock parts.

What industries do you serve?

We work with commercial construction, industrial, and specialized clients across a range of sectors, alongside our broader custom fabrication work. If your project needs fabricated steel built to specification, get in touch and we can confirm how we can help.

How is quality controlled during fabrication?

Quality is checked throughout the process, including dimensional verification and inspection of welds and finished components before delivery, so parts arrive ready to install and meet the project's requirements.
